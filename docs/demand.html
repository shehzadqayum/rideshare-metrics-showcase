Demand & shift timing (Sentinel)

A polling daemon that watches London's transport network and turns it into a surge forecast — so shift timing and positioning are chosen from data, not guesswork.

The idea. Ride-share pricing “surges” when demand outstrips drivers, and London demand is largely created by identifiable events: a Tube line down, 60,000 people leaving a football match, planes landing at Heathrow, rain. Sentinel polls Transport for London (ten endpoints), National Rail, football fixtures, Ticketmaster events and Met Office weather on a loop, scores each hour, and produces the forecast below. Two things are shown: the forecast the system generates, and the live feeds it is built from.

Surge forecast

Each hour of the day is scored per signal — aviation, football, events, transport disruption, weather — and combined into a composite. Darker = stronger predicted surge. This is a real generated example; open the full report for the complete write-up.
