Demand & shift timing (Sentinel)

A polling daemon that watches London's transport network and turns it into a surge forecast — so shift timing and positioning are chosen from data, not guesswork.

The idea. Ride-share pricing “surges” when demand outstrips drivers, and London demand is largely created by identifiable events: a Tube line down, 60,000 people leaving a football match, planes landing at Heathrow, rain. Sentinel polls ~30 endpoints across six providers — Transport for London, National Rail, airport arrivals, football fixtures, ticketed events and Met Office weather — scores each hour, and produces the forecast below. This page shows the forecast the system generates, the continuous logging session, and every metric of every daily capture.
