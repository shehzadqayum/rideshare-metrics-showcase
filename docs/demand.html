Demand & shift timing (Sentinel)

A polling daemon that watches London's transport network, on the theory that ride demand can be seen coming.

The idea. Ride-share pricing “surges” when demand outstrips drivers — and demand in London is largely created by identifiable events: a Tube line going down, 60,000 people leaving a football match, trains cancelled in the rain. Sentinel polls Transport for London's APIs (ten endpoints), National Rail feeds, football fixtures, Ticketmaster events and Met Office weather on a loop, logging everything to a database. The hypothesis under test: surge follows learnable patterns, so shift timing and positioning can be chosen from transport data rather than guesswork. Below is everything Sentinel actually captured: a continuous 90-minute logging run, and full multi-source snapshots across several days.
