Routes covered, with metrics

Every line on this map is a real GPS-tracked journey segment, reconstructed by the uber_screen pipeline from Driver-app screenshots and phone GPS logs.

What am I looking at? Ride-share work has three kinds of movement: en-route (driving to collect a passenger — unpaid), the trip itself (paid), and deadhead (repositioning with no job). The pipeline stitches each day's screenshots and GPS into these segments, then attaches the numbers: distance, duration, average speed, and what the trip paid. Hover any line to see them. Colouring by £ per mile shows instantly why trip selection matters — visually, not as a spreadsheet. Addresses are reduced to area level (e.g. “London N4”) for privacy.

Reading the map

A few things the routes make visible
  • Segment type — grey en-route lines show the unpaid approach to every pickup; the paid (blue) share of total driving is the “utilisation” the metrics page prices in.
  • £ per mile — short central London hops often out-earn long motorway runs per mile; the gradient makes the good and bad corridors obvious.
  • Coverage — 20 tracked days spanning inner and outer London, airports to suburbs, day and night shifts.
