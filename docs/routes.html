Routes covered, with metrics

Every line on this map is a real GPS-tracked journey segment, reconstructed by the uber_screen pipeline from Driver-app screenshots and phone GPS logs.

What am I looking at? Ride-share work has two kinds of movement per job: the en-route leg (driving to collect the passenger — unpaid) and the trip itself (paid). Each line here is one such segment, reconstructed by matching the phone's raw GPS track to the trip's start and end times, then tagged with its distance, duration, average speed and fare. Hover any line to see them. Colouring by £ per mile shows instantly why trip selection matters — visually, not as a spreadsheet. Addresses are reduced to area level (e.g. “London N4”) for privacy.
Colour by:
Played at the speed actually recorded by the GPS — traffic, stops and motorway are all real, and the clock shows the true time of day. Speeds are true multiples of real time: 1× is one second of driving per second, 60× is a minute per second. Idle waits between jobs are skipped.

Reading the map

A few things the routes make visible
  • Segment type — grey en-route lines show the unpaid approach to every pickup; the paid (blue) share of total driving is the “utilisation” the metrics page prices in.
  • £ per mile — short central London hops often out-earn long motorway runs per mile; the gradient makes the good and bad corridors obvious.
  • Coverage — spanning inner and outer London, airports to suburbs, day and night shifts.
  • Play a day — press ▶ Play a day below the map (or pick a specific day first) to watch a shift retrace itself at the real recorded speed: the car crawls in traffic and flies on the motorway, a live speedometer and the true time-of-day clock run alongside the trip count and earnings, and the idle waits between jobs are skipped.
  • Full screen — the ⛶ button top-left fills the screen and every control comes with it, as one instrument cluster: day navigation and the player across the foot (the day label opens a picker for all 31 days), the colour modes and their legend folding up out of that bar behind ⚙ Layers so nothing covers the map, and a live speedometer bottom-left showing current speed, the average for the job in hand beside the average for the whole shift so far — the gap between them is the unpaid approach — and the true date and time.
