EV running costs

The cost side of every profitability number on this site — parsed out of charging invoices by AI rather than typed in by hand.

Why it matters. An hourly rate “after costs” is only as honest as the cost data. Driving an electric vehicle for ride-share means dozens of charging sessions a month across different networks (Tesla Superchargers, Believ, Chargemaster/bp pulse, Smoov), each with its own invoice format — PDFs, app screenshots, VAT receipts. uber_charging feeds each document to Claude, which reads it directly (no brittle OCR templates) and emits a structured session record: kWh, cost, location, time. The aggregate — about £89/week — flows into the £430/week fixed-cost figure the metrics engine normalises against. Congestion-charge fees and idle-time penalties are caught too.
